# Shawarma Empire Art Style Guide

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent visual direction for **Shawarma Empire**. Every future asset, scene, UI element, character, building, decoration, animation, and promotional visual should follow this guide unless a later approved art direction document replaces it.

The target style is **Cozy Cartoon**: warm, welcoming, colorful, relaxing, family friendly, and designed first for readable Android mobile play.

## Art Vision

Shawarma Empire should look like a friendly neighborhood food marketplace that players want to revisit throughout the day. The world should feel comforting, optimistic, and full of gentle life without becoming visually noisy.

Core art goals:

- **Warm:** Use sunlight, golden highlights, wood tones, and food-inspired colors to create comfort.
- **Welcoming:** Make every stand, customer, button, and reward feel approachable and pleasant.
- **Colorful:** Use cheerful color variety while keeping gameplay information clear.
- **Relaxing:** Avoid harsh contrast, visual clutter, aggressive effects, or stressful presentation.
- **Family friendly:** Keep characters, environments, humor, and UI safe for all ages.
- **Mobile first:** Prioritize readable silhouettes, large shapes, clean contrast, and simple asset detail that works on small screens.

The art should support the game fantasy: starting with a humble shawarma stand and growing into a beloved food empire that feels alive, tasty, and easy to understand at a glance.

## Camera

The game should use a **top-down camera with a slight angle** so the player can clearly read the business layout, customers, queue, stand, and decorations.

Camera principles:

- Use a top-down view that shows floor space and customer movement clearly.
- Add a slight angle so objects have charm, depth, and recognizable fronts.
- Keep silhouettes readable even when assets are small on mobile screens.
- Avoid camera perspectives that hide important interactions behind buildings or props.
- Favor stable framing over dramatic camera movement.
- Make sure touch targets, UI, and active gameplay areas remain visually separate.

## Shapes

The entire game should be built from friendly, simplified shapes.

Shape language:

- Rounded corners.
- Oversized key features.
- Soft edges.
- Simplified geometry.
- Chunky props that read clearly at small sizes.
- Squash-friendly forms that can animate without feeling stiff.

Avoid tiny details that only work when zoomed in. If a player cannot recognize an asset quickly on a phone screen, simplify the shape before adding detail.

## Color Palette

The palette should feel warm, appetizing, and cheerful. Colors should help the player understand the scene while reinforcing the cozy food-market fantasy.

Primary palette direction:

- **Warm wood:** stalls, counters, crates, furniture, trim, and market structures.
- **Red:** shawarma branding, awnings, accents, buttons, tomato ingredients, and energetic highlights.
- **Cream:** bread, sauces, walls, cards, UI panels, and soft neutral backgrounds.
- **Green vegetables:** cucumbers, herbs, lettuce, plants, trees, and fresh food accents.
- **Golden lighting:** lantern glow, reward highlights, cooking warmth, evening market ambience, and upgrade celebrations.

Color rules:

- Prefer warm neutrals over cold grays.
- Use bright accents for rewards, active buttons, and important progression moments.
- Keep gameplay-critical colors consistent across the game.
- Maintain enough contrast for readability on mobile screens.
- Avoid palettes that feel dark, gritty, metallic, or sterile.

## Environment

Environments should feel like a cozy marketplace where shawarma, people, and small businesses belong naturally.

Environment direction:

- Cozy marketplace layout.
- Trees that soften the scene and add life.
- Lanterns that create warm golden pools of light.
- Flower pots near stalls, walkways, and entrances.
- Wooden stalls with friendly handmade charm.
- Small decorative props such as crates, baskets, rugs, chalkboards, plants, and food displays.

Environment principles:

- Decorations should frame gameplay, not block it.
- Background detail should be charming but less visually dominant than customers, stands, rewards, and UI.
- Market spaces should feel clean, safe, and inviting.
- Reuse modular props to support performance and consistent style.
- Each new location may have unique cultural or regional flavor, but it must still feel warm, readable, and family friendly.

## Characters

Characters should be expressive, instantly readable, and varied enough to make the market feel alive.

Character direction:

- Exaggerated proportions.
- Large heads.
- Expressive faces.
- Simple clothing.
- Many color variations.
- Friendly poses and approachable body language.

Character principles:

- Emotions should read clearly from a distance.
- Faces should use simple, bold shapes for eyes, brows, mouths, and cheeks.
- Clothing should be simplified, colorful, and easy to recolor.
- Customer variants should come from safe combinations of skin tones, hair shapes, clothing colors, accessories, and silhouettes.
- Avoid stereotypes, offensive caricatures, or designs that make any customer group feel mocked.
- Animation should make customers feel alive without distracting from the service loop.

## Buildings

Buildings should feel handcrafted, warm, and expandable as the player grows the empire.

Building direction:

- Wooden structures.
- Colorful awnings.
- Warm lights.
- Rounded windows, signs, counters, and trim.
- Clear fronts that communicate purpose quickly.

Building principles:

- The shawarma stand should always remain recognizable as the heart of the business.
- Upgrade visuals should be noticeable without changing the cozy identity.
- Larger restaurants and franchise locations should feel like natural evolutions of the original stand.
- Building silhouettes should remain readable from the gameplay camera.
- Use warm lighting to make shops look open, safe, and inviting.

## UI

UI should feel like part of the same cozy cartoon world while staying highly readable and touch-friendly.

UI direction:

- Rounded buttons.
- Soft shadows.
- Bright colors.
- Cream or warm panel backgrounds.
- Clear icon shapes.
- Large labels and numbers.
- Friendly feedback animations for taps, purchases, rewards, and unlocks.

UI principles:

- Design for one-handed Android play.
- Keep important buttons large and easy to tap.
- Use consistent button colors for primary actions, secondary actions, locked states, and premium actions.
- Avoid visual clutter around currency, upgrades, recipes, and customer orders.
- Use soft shadows for depth, not heavy dark outlines.
- Make disabled or unaffordable states clear without making them feel harsh.

## Animation Principles

Animation should make the game feel juicy, satisfying, and alive while preserving performance on low-end Android devices.

Animation direction:

- Squash and stretch.
- Overshoot.
- Bounce.
- Small idle movement.

Animation principles:

- Taps should respond immediately with a soft scale or bounce.
- Purchases and rewards should feel satisfying through quick pop, sparkle, coin, or glow feedback.
- Customers should have subtle idle motion so queues feel alive.
- Cooking and serving should use readable anticipation and completion beats.
- Upgrade reveals should include small celebratory motion without becoming disruptive.
- Keep animations short, loop-friendly, and inexpensive to render.

## Things to Avoid

The following directions do not belong in Shawarma Empire's permanent visual identity:

- Realistic textures.
- Dark colors as the dominant mood.
- Hard edges.
- Military style.
- Cyberpunk.
- Pixel art.
- Gritty realism.
- Horror, dystopian, or aggressive themes.
- Excessive visual noise.
- Tiny high-detail assets that are unreadable on mobile.
- Effects that hide customers, food, rewards, or UI.

## Asset Review Checklist

Before adding or approving any future visual asset, confirm that it:

- Matches the Cozy Cartoon style.
- Feels warm, welcoming, colorful, relaxing, and family friendly.
- Reads clearly on a small mobile screen.
- Uses rounded, oversized, soft, simplified shapes.
- Fits the warm wood, red, cream, green vegetable, and golden lighting palette direction.
- Supports the cozy marketplace fantasy.
- Avoids realistic, dark, hard-edged, military, cyberpunk, or pixel-art aesthetics.
- Improves clarity or charm without adding unnecessary clutter.
