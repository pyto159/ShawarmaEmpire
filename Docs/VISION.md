# Vision

**Shawarma Empire** should grow from a simple shawarma stand into a warm, satisfying, and long-lasting idle tycoon experience where every tap, upgrade, and return visit makes the player feel closer to building a beloved food empire.

The ultimate goal is to create a mobile-first game that is easy to understand in seconds, rewarding to revisit throughout the day, and deep enough to support weeks, months, and years of expansion. Players should begin with a humble food truck, serve happy customers, improve their operation, and eventually manage a recognizable shawarma brand across cities, food courts, restaurants, franchises, events, and global competition.

Every future system should support this fantasy: a friendly business that is always growing, always improving, and always giving the player a clear reason to come back.

# Player Experience

## First Minute

The first minute should feel immediate, clear, and welcoming. The player should understand that customers want shawarma, serving them earns coins, and coins can be spent to make the business better.

The player should feel:

- Curious about the stand and customers.
- Confident about what to do next.
- Rewarded quickly after their first action.
- Delighted by visible movement, sound, and feedback.
- Relaxed rather than pressured.

There should be no confusion, clutter, or heavy explanation. The first minute should communicate the core loop through motion, readable UI, and satisfying rewards.

## First Session

The first session should prove that the business can grow. The player should complete a few meaningful upgrades, see the stand become more efficient, and leave with a clear next goal.

The player should feel:

- Smart for improving the operation.
- Motivated by visible coin growth.
- Comfortable with the pace of play.
- Excited to unlock the next upgrade, recipe, or helper.
- Assured that short sessions are enough to make progress.

By the end of the first session, the player should understand the main promise of the game: return often, make improvements, and watch the shawarma empire expand.

## First Day

The first day should turn the game from a novelty into a habit. The player should return multiple times, collect progress, spend earnings, and notice that each check-in creates momentum.

The player should feel:

- That the game respects their time.
- That progress continues between sessions.
- That upgrades are becoming more impactful.
- That new goals are appearing before old goals become stale.
- That the shawarma business is becoming personally meaningful.

The first day should establish trust. The game should feel generous, readable, and fair, with no frustration caused by unclear systems, aggressive interruptions, or slow early pacing.

## First Week

The first week should reveal the larger structure of the empire. The player should see a path beyond the starting stand through recipes, automation, locations, events, or other expansion goals.

The player should feel:

- Invested in long-term growth.
- Proud of how far the business has come.
- Interested in optimizing upgrades and earnings.
- Excited by new content and future unlocks.
- Comfortable returning for fast sessions rather than feeling obligated to grind.

The first week should balance familiarity with discovery. The player should still recognize the simple core loop while seeing enough variety to believe the game can keep growing.

## Long-Term Play

Long-term play should feel like managing a successful, evolving food empire. The player should always have meaningful goals at different scales: a quick upgrade, a new recipe, a better location, a limited-time event, a franchise milestone, or a global ranking target.

The player should feel:

- Loyal to their growing shawarma brand.
- Rewarded for consistent returns.
- Motivated by visible expansion and prestige.
- Free to play casually without falling behind harshly.
- Excited when updates add new places, foods, challenges, and celebrations.

Long-term systems should create aspiration without frustration. The game should remain relaxing and understandable even as the empire becomes larger.

# Core Principles

## Simple

The core loop should always be easy to explain: serve customers, earn coins, buy upgrades, and grow the business. New systems should add value without hiding the main goal.

## Relaxing

The game should feel calm and pleasant. Pressure, timers, alerts, and event goals should be used carefully so they motivate without creating stress.

## Rewarding

Every session should produce a reward. Rewards may be coins, upgrades, unlocks, animations, sounds, new goals, or visible business improvements.

## Mobile-first

All design decisions should assume Android mobile play first. Interfaces should be readable on small screens, interactions should be touch-friendly, and performance should support smooth play on low-end devices.

## One-handed

The game should be comfortable to play with one hand. Important actions should be easy to reach, understand, and repeat without precise input.

## Fast sessions

Players should be able to make meaningful progress in short sessions. A quick check-in should feel complete, useful, and satisfying.

## Visible progression

Growth should be visible through numbers, UI, animation, customer flow, stand improvements, new locations, and stronger rewards. Players should see what changed and why it matters.

## No frustration

The game should avoid unclear requirements, surprise interruptions, punishing waits, excessive complexity, and upgrades that feel meaningless. If the player has a goal, the path toward it should be understandable.

# Design Philosophy

Every upgrade should feel impactful. When the player spends currency, the result should be noticeable through faster service, higher income, better capacity, improved automation, new visuals, or clearer strategic value.

Every session should provide progress. Even short visits should let the player collect, improve, unlock, prepare, or move closer to a goal.

The player should always have a meaningful next goal. The game should present goals at multiple distances so there is always something immediate to do and something larger to anticipate.

Avoid unnecessary complexity. Systems should be introduced only when they strengthen the core fantasy of building a shawarma empire. Complexity should come from interesting choices and satisfying growth, not from confusing menus, hidden rules, or overlapping currencies.

Future development should favor clarity over feature count. A small, polished system that makes the player smile is more valuable than a large system that makes the game harder to understand.

# Visual Identity

Shawarma Empire should look warm, friendly, and modern. The visual style should make the food business feel inviting, clean, and full of life.

Visual direction:

- Warm colors that suggest fresh food, sunlight, spices, and comfort.
- Friendly atmosphere with cheerful customers and approachable environments.
- Modern food truck aesthetic that can grow into larger restaurants and branded locations.
- Rounded UI that feels soft, readable, and touch-friendly.
- Readable fonts that work well on small mobile screens.
- Juicy animations that make serving, earning, upgrading, and unlocking feel satisfying.
- Clean interface that keeps important information visible without overwhelming the player.

The art should support quick comprehension. Players should instantly understand where customers are, what can be upgraded, how much currency they have, and what changed after an action.

# Audio Direction

Audio should make the world feel alive while keeping the experience relaxing and pleasant for repeated mobile sessions.

Audio direction:

- Relaxing music that supports calm, low-pressure play.
- Street ambience that makes each location feel active and welcoming.
- Cooking sounds that reinforce the shawarma theme and make service feel tangible.
- Satisfying coin sounds that make earnings feel rewarding without becoming annoying.
- Positive feedback sounds for upgrades, unlocks, milestones, and completed goals.

Sound should provide feedback without clutter. Players may play with low volume or no sound, so audio should enhance clarity but never be required to understand progress.

# Expansion Vision

Shawarma Empire should be built to expand over time while preserving the simple, relaxing core loop.

Long-term expansion may include:

- New cities with distinct visual themes, customer types, and business goals.
- Food courts that introduce higher traffic, shared spaces, and location-based upgrades.
- Restaurants that evolve the food truck fantasy into larger service operations.
- Franchise management where players oversee multiple branches and brand-wide improvements.
- Special events built around festivals, limited recipes, bonus goals, and celebration themes.
- Seasonal updates that refresh visuals, rewards, and progression opportunities.
- Global rankings that let players compare growth, event performance, or franchise success.

Expansion should feel additive, not disruptive. New content should give players fresh goals while keeping the game understandable for returning players. The empire can become large, but the heart of the experience should remain simple: serve great shawarma, make customers happy, earn rewards, and keep growing.
